Personal Project Management Application: Creation/Initiation

By: Philip Parks | Published: June 15, 2025

Introduction

I have begun the paperwork for my first major personal project, a Project Management Application. I decided to make my first major project something that I would be able to use for the rest of my professional career. It will demonstrate my ability to follow the project management lifecycle along with the software development lifecycle.

Creation Phase

The creation phase of the project management lifecycle consists of forming a business plan. To put it simply, a business plan answers a business problem or question. The business problem I am addressing with this business plan is the need of a project management application. While there are many other competitors out there that can fit the bill, developing an internal solution to project management will not only give me a tool that I can use going forward, but will also allow be to demonstrate my ability to follow the project management, and software development lifecycle.

Initiation Phase

The initiation phase of the project management lifecycle consists of defining the goals of the project, identifying stakeholders, developing the business case, creating a project charter, and other administrative tasks. While many of these tasks are not necissarily needed when working on a project alone, I still created all the documentation for the initiation phase. This allows me to have a template for future projects and for reference as I am working on the content of the application.

Conclusion

I plan on working on this project every Sunday for at least 2 hours. I have a tentative timeline and will be publishing the documentation during the respective phase of the project management lifecycle and according to my communication plan.
